<p><p>Baghouses are air pollution control systems used to capture and remove particulate matter from industrial processes. There are three main types of baghouses: </p><p>1) Mechanical Shaker Bag Filter: This type uses a motorized mechanism to shake the bags and dislodge the accumulated dust, which falls into a collection hopper, making it suitable for less demanding applications. </p><p>2) Reverse Air Bag Filter: In this type, bags are cleaned by backwashing with compressed air in the opposite direction of the gas flow. It is effective for high-temperature applications but requires significant energy consumption. </p><p>3) Pulse Jet Bag Filter: This type uses bursts of compressed air to clean the bags, providing efficient cleaning while maintaining low energy consumption. It is widely used in various industries due to its versatility and ability to handle high dust loads.</p></p>

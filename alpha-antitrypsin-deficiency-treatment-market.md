<p><p>Alpha-antitrypsin deficiency is a genetic disorder that affects the liver and lungs, causing an increased risk of developing lung diseases such as chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) and liver diseases such as cirrhosis. The main goal of treatment for alpha-antitrypsin deficiency is to manage the symptoms and slow down the progress of the disease.</p><p>Treatment options for alpha-antitrypsin deficiency may include replacement therapy, which involves infusing alpha-antitrypsin protein derived from human plasma into the patient's bloodstream. This helps to increase the levels of alpha-antitrypsin in the blood and protect the lungs from damage. Other treatment approaches may include medications to manage symptoms and complications, lung transplantation, and lifestyle modifications such as avoiding smoking and environmental pollutants.</p><p>The future outlook of the alpha-antitrypsin deficiency treatment market is expected to be promising.

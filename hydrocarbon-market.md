Aliphatic hydrocarbons are characterized by straight or branched chains of carbon atoms, often found in petroleum products and used as solvents, fuels, or raw materials in the production of plastics. Aromatic hydrocarbons, on the other hand, have a ring-like structure with alternating double bonds, commonly found in crude oil and used in the production of dyes, pharmaceuticals, and solvents.
